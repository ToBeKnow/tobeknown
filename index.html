About

I've always had people around me. Friends, close friends, best friends — the kind you think will be there forever.

And then life moves. You change cities, you change versions of yourself. And somewhere along the way, people who were everything to you become someone you used to know. No fight. No explanation. Just... gone.

I couldn't figure out why. And I realized I'd never really tried. Nobody does. We talk endlessly about romantic love — why it works, why it doesn't, how to heal when it ends. But friendship? We just let it happen. Or let it go.

And yet some friendships survive everything. Distance, years, silence. They start at 40, or at 7, or in a bar on a random Tuesday. They hold.

Why? How? What is actually happening between two people when they choose each other — or stop?

I don't have answers. I have questions. And I want your stories.

Stories
How did you meet?

We met in line at a coffee shop. She was reading the same book I had in my bag. We talked for four hours and never ordered anything. That was eleven years ago.

— Anonymous, San Antonio

What happened?

We stopped being friends the way you stop believing in something. Slowly, then all at once. I still don't have a word for what it was.

— J., 34
